My PhD project is funded by EU H2020 project DESTination Rail. This research seeks to facilitate the railway infrastructure managers in taking maintenance decisions based on explicit data grounds. A maintenance decision require consideration of a number of factors, for instance (a) Effect on network operations, (b) Cost of maintenance and overall life cycle cost, (c) Criticality of an object in network , (d) Risk of failures and many others. A maintenance decision based on the critical evaluation of these factors will improve the network availability as well as result in a cost-effective maintenance activity. Therefore, processing of such maintenance decision questions foster three main requirements (see Jardine et al. (2006) :

* <b>Need of data values</b> of various factors, e.g. assets' conditions, available budgets and cost limits, network schedule, etc.
* <b>Need of a decision support model</b> for analysis of data and comparison of various maintenance alternatives.
* <b>Need of computerized decision support tool</b> to minimize the chances of human errors and to improve the visualization.

The aim of this research is to develop a decision support tool based on comprehensive model that provides interactive visualization of various maintenance alternatives and recommend the best maintenance option based on assets' condition, minimal effect on network operations and optimal cost values.  A multi-asset information management system and a GIS-based tool has been developed to fulfil the data demands of decision model. Beside the results obtained from life cycle analysis and risk assessment, I am researching the potential of using methods multi-criteria decision analysis a) for the optimization of constraint resources and 2) to include the subjective judgments of decision maker(s) in decision-making. Furthermore, in order to make use of current business process of railway, we have developed a machine learning model to predict the need of maintenance as well as specific maintenance treatment. 

<b>WG 2: Performance goals (Quality Specification of roadway bridges COST TU Action 1406)</b>
I am an active participant in COST TU Action 1406 working group 2. The objective is to identify and define the set of performance goals varying to technical, environmental, economic, and social factors. Moreover, it also include the description on how to quantify performance goals from performance indicators.
